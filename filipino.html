Popular Filipino Dishes

Adobo

Description: A savory dish made with pork or chicken marinated in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices, then simmered until tender.

Ingredients:
  • 1 kg pork or chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp peppercorns
  • Salt to taste
Preparation Steps:
  1. Marinate the meat in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and peppercorns for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Simmer the mixture in a pot over medium heat for 45 minutes, or until the meat is tender.
  3. Add bay leaf and adjust seasoning to taste.
  4. Serve with steamed rice.

Sinigang na Baboy

Description: A tangy tamarind-based soup with pork, vegetables, and a variety of spices.

Ingredients:
  • 1 kg pork belly (cut into pieces)
  • 1 onion (quartered)
  • 2 tomatoes (quartered)
  • 1 pack sinigang mix (or fresh tamarind)
  • 2 long green chili peppers
  • 1 eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 2 okra (sliced)
  • 1 radish (sliced)
  • Salt to taste
Preparation Steps:
  1. Boil pork with onions and tomatoes until tender.
  2. Add sinigang mix (or tamarind) and simmer for another 10 minutes.
  3. Add vegetables and cook for another 5 minutes until tender.
  4. Season with salt and serve hot.

Pancit Canton

Description: A stir-fried noodle dish made with vegetables, meat, and a savory sauce.

Ingredients:
  • 200g pancit canton noodles
  • 1/2 lb chicken breast (shredded)
  • 1/2 lb pork (sliced thinly)
  • 2 carrots (julienned)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup oyster sauce
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup snow peas
  • 1/2 cup bell pepper (sliced)
  • 2 tbsp oil
Preparation Steps:
  1. Cook pancit canton noodles according to package instructions.
  2. Stir-fry pork and chicken in oil until cooked through.
  3. Add vegetables, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and chicken broth. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Add noodles and stir-fry until well mixed and heated through.
  5. Serve hot with a squeeze of calamansi (Philippine lime).

Lechon Kawali

Description: Deep-fried crispy pork belly served with a dipping sauce of vinegar and soy sauce.

Ingredients:
  • 1 kg pork belly (cut into chunks)
  • 1 onion (quartered)
  • 1 head garlic (crushed)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying
Preparation Steps:
  1. Boil pork belly with onions, garlic, bay leaves, salt, and pepper until tender.
  2. Drain and allow the pork to cool before deep-frying in hot oil until crispy.
  3. Serve with a dipping sauce of vinegar, soy sauce, and crushed peppercorns.

Halo-Halo

Description: A famous Filipino dessert made with a mixture of crushed ice, fruits, jellies, and leche flan.

Ingredients:
  • Shaved ice
  • Sweetened beans (red mung beans, garbanzo beans)
  • Sweetened banana (saba)
  • Leche flan
  • Ube halaya (purple yam jam)
  • Jelly (nata de coco or coconut gel)
  • Evaporated milk
  • Sugar (optional)
Preparation Steps:
  1. Layer the ingredients in a tall glass: start with the sweetened beans, banana, leche flan, ube, and jelly.
  2. Add a scoop of shaved ice on top.
  3. Drizzle with evaporated milk and sprinkle sugar to taste.
  4. Mix well before eating for a refreshing treat!
